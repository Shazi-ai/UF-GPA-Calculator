About GPA Calculation

What is GPA?

Grade Point Average (GPA) is a standardized measurement of academic achievement in U.S. universities and colleges. It's calculated by dividing the total number of grade points earned by the total number of credit hours attempted.

GPA Scale at UF

The University of Florida uses a 4.0 GPA scale. Each letter grade corresponds to a specific grade point value:

Letter Grade Grade Points Percentage
A 4.0 93-100%
A- 3.67 90-92%
B+ 3.33 87-89%
B 3.0 83-86%
B- 2.67 80-82%
C+ 2.33 77-79%
C 2.0 73-76%

D 1.0 63-66%
D- 0.67 60-62%

How GPA is Calculated

To find your total grade points, multiply the grade point value of each grade by the number of credit hours for that course, then add these values together.

GPA Requirements at UF

  • Minimum GPA required for graduation: 2.0
  • Dean's List: 3.5 or higher
  • President's Honor Roll: 4.0
  • Many UF majors have specific GPA requirements for admission to the program

Different Types of GPA

Term GPA

The GPA earned in a single semester or term.

Cumulative GPA

The overall GPA for all semesters/terms combined.

Major GPA

The GPA for courses in your major only.

Help & FAQ

How to Use the GPA Calculator

  1. Click the Add Course button to add a new course row.
  2. Enter the course name (optional).
  3. Enter the number of credit hours for the course.
  4. Select the grade you received (or expect to receive).
  5. The calculator will automatically update your GPA.
  6. To remove a course, click the trash icon next to the course row.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a good GPA at UF?

At UF, a GPA of 3.0 or higher is generally considered good. A GPA of 3.5 or higher may qualify you for the Dean's List, and a 4.0 GPA may qualify you for the President's Honor Roll.

How do I calculate my cumulative GPA?

Switch to the "Cumulative GPA" tab, enter your current cumulative GPA, total credits earned, and your new semester information.

Do pass/fail courses affect my GPA?

No, pass/fail courses (marked as S/U at UF) do not count toward your GPA calculation.

How can I raise my GPA?

To raise your GPA, you need to earn higher grades in future courses. The more credit hours you've already completed, the harder it becomes to significantly change your cumulative GPA.

What GPA do I need to graduate?

At UF, you generally need a minimum 2.0 cumulative GPA to graduate, though some majors have higher requirements.

UF GPA Information

University of Florida GPA System

The University of Florida uses a 4.0 grading scale. Your GPA is important for many reasons:

  • Academic standing and continued enrollment
  • Admission to limited-access majors
  • Eligibility for scholarships and financial aid
  • Qualification for academic honors
  • Graduate school applications
  • Job applications after graduation

Important UF GPA Thresholds

Achievement GPA Requirement

Note: Specific college and department requirements may vary.

GPA Calculation Details

The University of Florida calculates several different types of GPAs:

  • Term GPA: Grades earned in a single semester
  • UF GPA: All grades earned at UF
  • Overall GPA: Includes transfer credits
  • Major GPA: Only courses in your major
  • Upper Division GPA: Only 3000-4000 level courses
